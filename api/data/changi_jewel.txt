Changi Airport has four main passenger terminals: Terminal 1, Terminal 2, Terminal 3, and Terminal 4. Each terminal is designed with a unique concept while maintaining the signature Changi Experience.
Terminal 1 is the oldest terminal at Changi Airport, opened in 1981, but has been extensively renovated and upgraded over the years.
Terminal 2 was opened in 1990 and features a tropical garden theme with lush greenery throughout the terminal.
Terminal 3 opened in 2008 and is known for its butterfly garden, the world's first butterfly habitat in an airport.
Terminal 4 is the newest terminal, opened in 2017, featuring a boutique design with a showcase of local culture and heritage.
Changi Airport's Terminal 5 is currently under construction and is expected to be one of the largest airport terminals in the world when completed.
Terminal 4 at Changi Airport offers various facilities for transit passengers including free movie theaters, a heritage zone showcasing Peranakan culture, numerous dining options, duty-free shopping, free Wi-Fi, charging stations, rest areas, shower facilities, and a transit hotel.
Changi Airport serves over 100 airlines flying to more than 380 cities in about 100 countries and territories worldwide.
Changi Airport has consistently been voted as the World's Best Airport by Skytrax for multiple years, known for its excellent facilities, efficiency, and passenger experience.
Jewel Changi Airport is a nature-themed entertainment and retail complex linked to Terminal 1, 2, and 3. It features the world's tallest indoor waterfall, the Rain Vortex, which stands at 40 meters tall.
Changi Airport handles over 60 million passengers annually (pre-COVID) and has a capacity to handle 85 million passengers per year across its four terminals.
